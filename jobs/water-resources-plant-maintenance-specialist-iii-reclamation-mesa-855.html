thereafter as needed. &nbsp; &nbsp; Water Resources Plant Maintenance Specialist I ($47,757.84 – $66,070.16 annually) A Water Resources Plant Maintenance Specialist I is a one-year assignment where incumbents function as a trainee and are responsible for learning how to and begin to maintain the City of Mesa’s water reclamation or water treatment plants (depending on assignment) . &nbsp;An employee in this class accompanies and observes a lead person, specifically the Water Resources Plant Maintenance Specialist II and/or III class, thus performing daily activities and assisting in performing entry-level work. &nbsp;As the incumbent demonstrates the ability to perform the full range of the proposed Water Resources Plant Maintenance Specialist I duties, the complexity and difficulty of assignments will increase. &nbsp;During this one-year assignment the incumbent must meet requirements and expectations outlined in the criteria-based promotion document. &nbsp;In addition, the incumbent must complete these requirements and proceed to the Water Resources Plant Maintenance Specialist II classification within the one-year time frame. &nbsp;This class performs related duties as required.&nbsp; Water Reclamation Plant Assignment: &nbsp; Incumbents may be assigned to either the Southeast, Northwest, or Greenfield Water Reclamation Plants. &nbsp;Entry-level work will consist of plant maintenance work. &nbsp;Some examples of duties learned and performed include:&nbsp; installing, maintaining, and repairing equipment and facilities including, but not limited to:&nbsp; bar screens, compactors, degritters, mixers, pumps, compressors, pipes, blowers, odor control devices, centrifuges, ultraviolet (UV) equipment, chemical metering pumps, sludge hoppers, conveyor systems, boilers, biogas systems, waste gas flares, valves, gear boxes, and tanks.&nbsp; Incumbents also learn the basics of plant operations involving primary treatment, secondary treatment, activated sludge processes, filtration, disinfection, sludge dewatering, and digestion.&nbsp; This class is distinguished from the Water Resources Plant Maintenance Specialist II class by its responsibility for performing entry-level plant maintenance duties while learning to perform journey-level plant maintenance.&nbsp;
